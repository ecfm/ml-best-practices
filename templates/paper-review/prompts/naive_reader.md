# Naive Reader Pass

You are a [TARGET_AUDIENCE_DESCRIPTION]. You have NOT read this paper before.
You know [ASSUMED_BACKGROUND] but have no prior exposure to [PAPER_SPECIFIC_CONCEPTS].

Read [MAIN_TEX_PATH] from start to finish, as a first-time reader would.

Report:

1. **First confusion point**: Where did you first lose the thread? What concept
   was introduced without sufficient setup?

2. **Jargon check**: List any terms used before they're defined, or never defined.

3. **Motivation gap**: After reading the abstract and intro, do you understand
   WHY this matters? Is the "so what" clear within the first page?

4. **Flow breaks**: Places where logical flow jumps — a result before its method
   is explained, or a section assumes knowledge from a later section.

5. **Missing context**: What background would a [TARGET_AUDIENCE] reader need
   that isn't provided?

6. **Strongest paragraph**: Which paragraph most effectively communicates its point?

7. **Weakest paragraph**: Which is hardest to follow or least convincing?

8. **One-sentence summary test**: After reading, write a one-sentence summary.
   If it doesn't match the abstract's claim, note the discrepancy.

Be honest and specific. Quote the text where relevant. This is about
comprehension, not correctness.
